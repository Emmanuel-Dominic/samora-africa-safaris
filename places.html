Places to Visit

Discover amazing destinations

Uganda

Bwindi Impenetrable Forest

UNESCO World Heritage Site, home to over 500 mountain gorillas - nearly half the world's remaining population.

Queen Elizabeth NP

Home to tree-climbing lions, boat trips on Kazinga Channel, and one of the world's largest hippo concentrations.

Rwenzori Mountains

The legendary "Mountains of the Moon" offering challenging climbs and stunning alpine scenery.

Lake Bunyonyi

Africa's second deepest lake, dotted with 29 islands, perfect for canoeing and relaxation.

Kidepo Valley NP

Uganda's most isolated park, offering pure wilderness and rare wildlife including cheetahs and ostriches.

Murchison Falls NP

Uganda's largest park featuring the powerful Nile waterfall cascading 43 meters through a 7-meter gorge.

Fort Portal

Gateway to chimpanzee trekking in Kibale Forest, surrounded by crater lakes and tea plantations.

Sipi Falls

Three stunning waterfalls on Mount Elgon's slopes, offering hiking and coffee plantation tours.

Jinja & Source of Nile

Adventure capital of East Africa, famous for white-water rafting and the source of the River Nile.

Lake Victoria

Africa's largest freshwater lake, offering boat trips, fishing, and island exploration opportunities.

Kampala City

Uganda's vibrant capital featuring cultural sites, markets, and the historic Kasubi Tombs.

Rwanda

Volcanoes National Park

Part of the Virunga Massif with magnificent volcanic scenery and endangered mountain gorillas.

Lake Kivu

Rwanda's largest lake offering kayaking, boat rides, and serene shoreline resorts for relaxation.

Kigali City

Rwanda's clean and modern capital featuring the Genocide Memorial and vibrant cultural experiences.

Nyungwe Forest NP

Africa's largest protected mountain forest with chimpanzees, canopy walkways, and 300 bird species.

Akagera National Park

Rwanda's only savannah park with Big Five wildlife, scenic lakes, and diverse ecosystems.
