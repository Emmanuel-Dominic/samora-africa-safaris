Information

Important information for your safari

Visa Information
Uganda

Most visitors require a visa to enter Uganda. You can obtain a visa on arrival at Entebbe International Airport or apply for an e-visa online. The visa costs approximately $50 USD and is valid for 90 days.

Rwanda

Rwanda also requires a visa for most nationalities. You can apply for an e-visa online (recommended) or obtain one on arrival. The cost is approximately $50 USD. Processing takes 2-3 working days for e-visas.

Check with your embassy for specific requirements based on your nationality.

Health and Safety
Vaccinations
  • Yellow Fever - Recommended/Required
  • Typhoid - Recommended
  • Hepatitis A - Recommended
  • Malaria Prophylaxis - Recommended for lower altitude areas
  • Tetanus - Standard immunization
General Safety

Uganda and Rwanda are generally safe destinations for tourists. Our guides are experienced and knowledgeable about safety protocols. Stay alert in urban areas, avoid displaying valuables, and follow your guide's instructions during wildlife encounters.

Consult your doctor 4-6 weeks before travel for personalized health recommendations.

Climate and Weather
Best Seasons

Dry Seasons: June-September and December-February offer the best conditions for game viewing with clear skies and manageable temperatures.

Rainy Seasons

March-May and October-November: Lush green landscapes, fewer tourists, and unique bird watching opportunities. Roads may be challenging but wildlife is still abundant.

Temperature

Average temperatures range from 15-25°C (59-77°F). Higher altitudes are cooler. Bring layers for early morning game drives.

Currency and Payments
Uganda

Currency: Ugandan Shilling (UGX). ATMs are available in Kampala. US Dollars are widely accepted. Credit cards are accepted in major cities and tourist areas.

Rwanda

Currency: Rwandan Franc (RWF). ATMs are available in Kigali. US Dollars are widely accepted. Credit cards work in major establishments.

Inform your bank of your travel dates to avoid card blocks.

Communication
Internet and Phone

Mobile networks are good in main towns. 4G/LTE services are available. Purchase a local SIM card for affordable data. WiFi is available at most hotels and lodges.

International Dialing

Uganda: +256 | Rwanda: +250. Local SIM cards typically cost $1-2 with reasonable data plans.

Packing Essentials
Clothing
  • Neutral colors (khaki, green, brown)
  • Long pants and long sleeves
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Warm layers for mornings
  • Rain jacket
  • Hat and scarf
Accessories
  • High SPF sunscreen
  • Insect repellent
  • Binoculars
  • Camera/telephoto lens
  • Flashlight/headlamp
  • Medications
Cultural Etiquette
  • Greetings: A handshake and greeting are important. Always greet people you meet.
  • Photography: Always ask permission before photographing people or sacred sites.
  • Respect: Show respect for local customs and traditions.
  • Dress Code: Dress modestly, especially when visiting villages and religious sites.
  • Language: Learning a few words in the local language (Luganda in Uganda, Kinyarwanda in Rwanda) is greatly appreciated.
  • Tipping: Tips are appreciated but not mandatory. Round up bills or tip 10% for good service.
