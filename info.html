Important information for your safari

Visa Information
Uganda

All persons planning to travel to Uganda from non-visa-exempt countries for tourism, holidays, business, or seeking medical care must apply for and obtain a visa. The Ministry of Internal Affairs, through the Directorate of Citizenship and Immigration Control, issues the Ugandan visa online. We recommend applying at least two months before the entry date, as approval typically takes three working days. The visa costs $50 USD and is valid for 90 days.

Rwanda

Anyone planning to travel to Rwanda from visa-exempt countries must apply for an entry visa online or in person upon arrival at Kigali International Airport and all land borders. The Rwandan Ordinary/Tourist Single Entry Visa costs $50 USD. Visa processing typically requires 3-7 business days.

East African Tourist Visa

This multiple-entry visa ($100 USD) allows travelers to visit Uganda, Rwanda, and Kenya for tourism purposes. Entry must begin with the country that issued the visa.

Your passport must have a minimum validity of 6 months from the intended entrance date and at least two blank visa pages.

Health and Safety
Mandatory Vaccinations
  • Yellow Fever - Required for Uganda. A Yellow Fever Vaccination Certificate is required during the visa application and on arrival.
  • For Rwanda - Yellow fever certificate is mandatory for travelers from yellow fever-prone countries.
Recommended Vaccinations
  • Meningitis, Hepatitis B and A, Tetanus, and Typhoid
  • Malaria Prophylaxis - Uganda has mosquitoes that transmit malaria. Take prescribed medicine before, during, and after your trip.
  • Sleep under treated mosquito nets in addition to using malaria prophylaxis
General Safety

Uganda and Rwanda are safe destinations for tourists, and the respective governments remain committed to ensuring the safety and security of visitors. All major tourist destinations and national parks are well-secured and regularly patrolled by armed park rangers. In urban areas, stay alert, avoid openly displaying valuables, and follow your guide's instructions during wildlife encounters.

Before traveling, acquire personal travel insurance to cover emergency medical care and other insurable risks.

Climate and Weather
Dry Seasons (Best for Safaris)

Uganda experiences two dry seasons: December to February and June to August. During these months, the weather is dry and sunny, creating perfect conditions for wildlife safaris, hiking, and other adventures. Animals gather around water sources, making sightings frequent and highly rewarding.

Rainy Seasons

The rainy seasons occur from March to May and September to December. These seasons are characterized by lush vegetation, flowering flora, and increased water supplies, making them perfect for bird watching, photography, and experiencing the country's dynamic ecosystems at their best.

Temperature

Uganda experiences moderate temperatures ranging between 21°C (70°F) and 25°C (77°F) throughout the year. Higher regions such as the Rwenzori Mountains and southwestern Uganda have colder temperatures, while northern and eastern plains are often drier. The central part, including Kampala and Entebbe, has a generally warm and humid climate.

Currency and Payments
Uganda

Currency: Ugandan Shilling (UGX). ATMs are available in Kampala. US Dollars are widely accepted. Credit cards are accepted in major cities and tourist areas.

Rwanda

Currency: Rwandan Franc (RWF). ATMs are available in Kigali. US Dollars are widely accepted. Credit cards work in major establishments.

Inform your bank of your travel dates to avoid card blocks.

Communication
Internet and Phone

Mobile networks are good in main towns. 4G/LTE services are available. Purchase a local SIM card for affordable data. WiFi is available at most hotels and lodges.

International Dialing

Uganda: +256 | Rwanda: +250. Local SIM cards typically cost $1-2 with reasonable data plans.

Packing Essentials
Clothing
  • Neutral colors (khaki, green, brown)
  • Long pants and long sleeves
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Warm layers for mornings
  • Rain jacket
  • Hat and scarf
Accessories
  • High SPF sunscreen
  • Insect repellent
  • Binoculars
  • Camera/telephoto lens
  • Flashlight/headlamp
  • Medications
Cultural Etiquette
  • Greetings: A handshake and greeting are important. Always greet people you meet.
  • Photography: Always ask permission before photographing people or sacred sites.
  • Respect: Show respect for local customs and traditions.
  • Dress Code: Dress modestly, especially when visiting villages and religious sites.
  • Language: Learning a few words in the local language (Luganda in Uganda, Kinyarwanda in Rwanda) is greatly appreciated.
  • Tipping: Tips are appreciated but not mandatory. Round up bills or tip 10% for good service.
