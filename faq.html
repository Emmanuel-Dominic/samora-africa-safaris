Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions

The best time to visit is during the dry seasons: June to September and December to February. However, the lush green scenery during the rainy seasons (March-May and October-November) also offers excellent opportunities for wildlife viewing and fewer tourists.

Visa requirements depend on your nationality. Most visitors need a visa, which can be obtained on arrival or through an embassy. We recommend checking with your country's embassy for specific requirements. We can assist with visa documentation if needed.

Yellow fever vaccination is typically required or recommended. We also suggest vaccinations for typhoid, hepatitis A, and tetanus. Malaria prophylaxis is recommended for lower altitude areas. Consult your doctor at least 4-6 weeks before your trip.

Cancellations made more than 30 days before the safari receive a full refund minus a 10% processing fee. Cancellations 15-30 days before receive 50% refund. Cancellations within 15 days of the safari are non-refundable. Travel insurance is strongly recommended.

Tipping is not mandatory but appreciated. Guides typically receive $5-10 per day, drivers $3-5 per day, and accommodation staff $2-3 per day. It's a nice gesture to show appreciation for good service. Tips are not included in package prices.

Pack neutral-colored clothing (khaki, brown, green), comfortable walking shoes, sun protection (hat, sunscreen), binoculars, camera equipment, light layers for early morning drives, and insect repellent. Avoid bright colors as they can startle wildlife. A detailed packing list will be provided with your booking confirmation.

Yes, gorilla trekking is very safe. Our experienced guides strictly follow protocols to ensure both human and gorilla safety. Groups are small (maximum 8 people) and maintain safe distances from the gorillas. Treks are suitable for people of reasonable fitness levels.
